Enhancing the Efficiency of Coolants using Nano Materials

Led a team of 4 where we designed and formulated a “coolant that can effectively conduct more heat than conventional coolants”. Interested in the field of research and thermal engineering, me and my friends decided to work on thermal coolants that will improve the efficieny of thermal conductivity in electronics applications. With Industry 4.0 and automation on the rise, the involvement of electronics in manufacturing sector was surging every year. Considering the amount of coolants used to maintain these devices and servers that runs these machines needs more maintainence than the actual machines themselves. In order to control the enormous water wastage every year by these machines we decided to control them with high thermal efficient coolants that can be recycled to reduce cost. we infused CNT and glycol in different proportions using permutation and combination to find the ideal ratio for the application. Using ultra sonicator and KD2 thermal pro equipments we then tested the solution and found that the solution was 12% more conductive than conventional coolants like synthesized glycol (available in open market) and 34% more efficient than water. However, extensive research for paper publication was haulted due to covid.
